Notes from Technical writing course - Google

Last updated: 2025-09-07

Source: https://developers.google.com/tech-writing/one

Key takeaways:

  1. Make sure its easy to identify the nouns associated with your pronouns.
  2. Prefer active voice over passive voice.
  3. Aim for clear sentences. 3.1 Choose strong verbs 3.2 Reduce there is / there are 3.3 Minimize certain adjectives and verbs
  4. Aim for Short sentences. 4.1 Break up long sentences into shorter ones. 4.2 Convert some longer sentences to lists. 4.3 Eliminate or reduce extranoues words. 4.4 Reduce subordinate clauses. 4.5 Which v/s That - which is for non-important stuff, that is for important stuff. - place a comma before which, do not place a comma before that.

Examples of weak/strong verb

  • Weak: The exception occurs when dividing by zero.
    • Strong: Dividing by zero raises the exception.
  • Weak: This error message happens when..
    • Strong: The system generates this error when..
  • Weak: We are very carefuly to ensure…
    • Strong: We carefully ensure…

Clarify the sentences by picking more specific verbs

  • Compiler error occurs when you leave off a semicolon at the end of a statement.
    • Compiler raises an error, when a statement is missing a semicolon.
